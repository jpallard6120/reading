# Blindsight

## Verdict

Meh. It was acceptable, but felt like an edgy Shadowrun scenario. Its apparent depth was weakened by context-poor worldbuilding, and the vampire premise was as cringey as expected.

## What worked

- It was ultimately okay rather than a complete failure
- The world suggested depth

## What did not

- Edgy tone
- Large amounts of world-specific content introduced without context
- Worldbuilding that felt deep without allowing the reader to experience that depth
- The genetically resurrected vampire framing

## Reader comments

Before reading:

> Jukka Sarasti, a genetically resurrected vampire? Seems out of hard sci-fi and entering fantasy?

After reading:

> Blindsight, meh. It was OK but felt like a an edgy shadowrun scenario. World building was lacking, it feels deep, but with a ton of world specific content thrown at your face without any context, you can't really experience the depth. And as I predicted, the vampire thing was extra cringe.

## Recommendation implications

### Recommend more of

- Worldbuilding that supplies context when unfamiliar concepts appear
- Depth the reader can experience through the story

### Avoid

- Edgelord framing
- Genre bait that remains distracting after its scientific explanation
- Dense invented context without orientation
