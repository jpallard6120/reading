# Project Hail Mary

## Verdict

S-tier. Exceptionally fun and emotionally effective, especially in making Rocky so likable.

## What worked

- Solo-adventure structure
- Concrete problem-solving
- Strong momentum
- A convincing emotional bond with a very alien character
- Humor that supported the adventure

## What did not

- No book-specific objection was recorded

## Reader comments

Broader author observation:

> I feel like Weir is amazing for solo adventures, but a bit less for complex social structures building.

## Recommendation implications

### Recommend more of

- Competence-driven survival stories
- Solo adventures built around tangible constraints
- First-contact stories with emotional warmth
- Plausible technical problem-solving

### Do not assume

- All humorous science fiction will work equally well
- Weir's strengths in solo adventures automatically extend to complex societies
