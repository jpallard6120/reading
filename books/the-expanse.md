# The Expanse

## Verdict

Not great literature, but a fantastic story. The entire series was compelling enough to finish in roughly two months.

## What worked

- Strong narrative propulsion across a long series
- A coherent, immersive world
- A story good enough to outweigh literary limitations
- James Holden's irreverence and humor became a reference point for later recommendations

## What did not

- The prose and literary quality were not exceptional

## Reader comments

> OK, I cleared out the whole The Expanse series from beginning to end in what... 2 months? It was great. Is it litterature? No. Is it a freakin great story? Hell yes.

Later, when weighing another recommendation:

> What if I'd like some of the irreverence and humor of a James Holden again?

## Recommendation implications

### Recommend more of

- Long, propulsive stories in coherent worlds
- Serious settings with competent, irreverent characters
- Humor rooted in personality rather than references

### Do not assume

- Literary prestige is necessary for an excellent reading experience
- A long series is a drawback when the story maintains momentum
