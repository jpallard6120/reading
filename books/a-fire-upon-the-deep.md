# A Fire Upon the Deep

## Verdict

Liked it, with caveats. It took far too long to begin its central adventure, introduced important invented terms without timely explanations, and pushed technology so far into the future that it sometimes felt like fantasy.

## What worked

- The eventual adventure paid off enough for the book to be liked overall
- The large world and ideas were interesting once the story moved

## What did not

- Roughly twelve chapters before the central adventure began
- Extensive setup without enough useful orientation
- Important invented vocabulary introduced several chapters before explanation
- Skrodes and Skroderiders were repeatedly mentioned before they could be pictured
- Far-future technology that bordered on fantasy

## Reader comments

## Recommendation implications

### Recommend more of

- Books that begin moving early
- Worldbuilding that explains inventions when they become relevant
- Near- or medium-future technology with understandable constraints

### Avoid

- Long openings before the central story
- Invented nouns used repeatedly without enough context
- Technology so advanced that it becomes indistinguishable from fantasy
