# Children of Time

## Verdict

The risky humans-versus-spiders premise worked. It was not cringey, apart from perhaps a little at the end, and the spiders became sympathetic enough to root for.

## What worked

- A strange premise executed seriously enough to remain believable
- Alien characters that became emotionally compelling
- The book could shift sympathy toward the spiders

## What did not

- The ending may have edged slightly toward cringe, though it remained fine overall

## Reader comments

Before reading:

> Last ditch humans against spiders? Wow if this is pulled off it will be epic (if not it'll be extra cringe)

After reading:

> I'm done, it wasn't cringey (maybe the end a bit, but tbh it was still fine!), you almost rooted for the spiders at some point!

## Recommendation implications

### Recommend more of

- Strange premises with disciplined, earned execution
- Genuinely nonhuman perspectives that still create emotional investment

### Do not assume

- An initially cringey-sounding premise is fatal when the execution is rigorous
