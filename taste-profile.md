# Reading taste profile

This is a working model, not a set of permanent rules. Strong reactions to individual books are recorded in `books/`; this file keeps only the patterns that matter across recommendations.

## Strong preferences

- Page-turning stories with early momentum
- Worlds that feel coherent, inhabited, and understandable
- Plausible technology, preferably near- or medium-future
- Competent characters solving concrete problems
- Humor rooted in character rather than references or memes
- Clear prose that does not feel simplistic
- Big ideas presented through story rather than lectures

## Common failure modes

- Long openings before the central story begins
- Invented vocabulary introduced without enough context
- Far-future technology that feels indistinguishable from magic
- Shallow nerd-reference humor
- Manchild protagonists
- Elaborate concepts with weak character development
- Stories built around a premise that is difficult to believe
- Dense prose that makes recreational reading feel like work
